# Cover Letter Templates

Templates for initial submissions and resubmissions.

## Standard Initial Submission

```
Dear [Dr./Prof. Editor Name | Editors],

Please consider our manuscript entitled "[Full Manuscript Title]" for
publication in [Journal Name] as a [Research Article / Brief Communication /
Review / etc.].

[PARAGRAPH 2: Brief summary - 2-4 sentences describing what you did and
what you found. Focus on the key finding and its significance.]

[PARAGRAPH 3: Why this journal - 2-3 sentences explaining why your work
fits this journal's scope and readership. Be specific to the journal.]

[PARAGRAPH 4 (if needed): Any special circumstances - preprints, related
submissions, data availability, etc.]

We confirm that this work is original, has not been published previously,
and is not under consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the
manuscript and agree with its submission to [Journal Name].

[If requested] We suggest the following potential reviewers:
- [Name, Institution, Email] - [Brief justification]
- [Name, Institution, Email] - [Brief justification]
- [Name, Institution, Email] - [Brief justification]

[If relevant] We request that [Name] not be invited to review due to
[competing interests / recent collaboration / etc.].

We declare no conflicts of interest.

Thank you for considering our submission.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
[Your Title]
[Your Institution]
[Your Email]
[Your Phone]

On behalf of all authors:
[List all author names]
```

## Short Form (When Brevity Preferred)

```
Dear Editors,

We submit "[Manuscript Title]" for consideration in [Journal Name].

[One paragraph: What you did, what you found, why it matters. Keep to
3-5 sentences.]

This work is original, not published elsewhere, and not under
consideration at other journals. All authors approve this submission.
We declare no conflicts of interest.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
On behalf of all authors
```

## Resubmission After Revision

```
Dear [Dr./Prof. Editor Name | Editors],

We are pleased to resubmit our revised manuscript entitled "[Manuscript
Title]" (Manuscript ID: [ID number]) for further consideration in
[Journal Name].

We thank the editor and reviewers for their constructive feedback, which
has helped us improve the manuscript. We have carefully addressed all
comments, as detailed in our point-by-point response.

Key changes include:
- [Major change 1]
- [Major change 2]
- [Major change 3]

[If applicable] Please note: [any changes to authorship, data availability,
or other administrative matters].

We believe the revised manuscript addresses the reviewers' concerns and
is now suitable for publication in [Journal Name].

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
On behalf of all authors
```

## Resubmission After Rejection (Different Journal)

```
Dear Editors,

Please consider our manuscript entitled "[Manuscript Title]" for
publication in [New Journal Name] as a [Article Type].

[Standard paragraph 2: What you did and found]

[Standard paragraph 3: Why this journal fits]

[Optional: If relevant] A previous version of this manuscript was reviewed
at [Previous Journal]. We have incorporated feedback from that review,
including [brief summary of improvements]. We are happy to share the
previous reviews and our responses if helpful.

[Standard declarations]

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
```

## Transfer Submission

```
Dear Editors,

We are submitting our manuscript entitled "[Manuscript Title]" following
transfer from [Previous Journal] (Original ID: [ID]).

[Brief explanation of transfer circumstances, if helpful]

We have [revised the manuscript based on feedback / made no changes /
addressed specific concerns from the previous review].

[Standard declarations]

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
```

## Special Circumstances

### Preprint Posted

```
[Add to standard letter]

We note that a preprint version of this manuscript has been posted to
[bioRxiv/medRxiv/arXiv/etc.] (DOI: [DOI]). The submitted version contains
[updates/revisions/no changes] from the preprint.
```

### Related Manuscripts

```
[Add to standard letter]

We wish to inform you that a related manuscript by our group is currently
[under review at / accepted by] [Journal]. That work focuses on [brief
description], while the present manuscript [distinguishes itself by...].
We do not believe this constitutes duplicate publication, but we wanted
to bring it to your attention.
```

### Data Availability

```
[Add to standard letter]

Data and code supporting this manuscript are available at [repository]
(DOI: [DOI]). [Alternative: Data will be deposited upon acceptance at
[repository].]
```

### Requesting Specific Editor

```
Dear Editors,

[Standard opening]

Given the [interdisciplinary nature / specific focus] of our work, we
believe Dr. [Name] would be well-suited to handle this submission due
to their expertise in [area]. However, we are happy to have any
appropriate editor oversee the review process.

[Continue with standard letter]
```

### Excluding Reviewers

```
[Add to standard letter]

We respectfully request that the following individuals not be invited
to review this manuscript:

- Dr. [Name], [Institution]: [Reason - e.g., competing research group,
  recent collaboration, known disagreement on methodology]
```

## Tips for Effective Cover Letters

### Do:
- Address editor by name if known
- Be specific about your findings
- Explain journal fit concretely
- Keep to one page
- Proofread carefully

### Don't:
- Use generic language ("groundbreaking," "novel")
- Repeat the abstract
- Include figures or extensive data
- Make promises about impact
- Forget required statements

### Journal-Specific Adjustments:
- Check if specific format is required
- Note any required statements
- Include requested information (reviewer suggestions, etc.)
- Match the journal's style and formality
